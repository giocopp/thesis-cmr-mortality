FE STRUCTURE DIAGNOSTIC
Why month-year FE is the appropriate specification

CONTEXT:
  Deiana et al. (2024): week-of-year FE (52 cells, seasonality only)
  Zambiasi & Albarosa (2025): month-of-year FE (12 cells, seasonality only)
  This paper: month-year FE (~96 cells, seasonality + year-specific levels)

TEST 1: Post-MoU death drop is uniform across SWH terciles
  Deaths dropped ~60% at all SWH levels — a level shift, not gradient.

TEST 2: FE comparison (2014-2021)
  Month-of-year only (Zambiasi)           b3 = +0.160  p = 0.4525
  Week-of-year only (Deiana)              b3 = +0.251  p = 0.1808
  Month-of-year + Year FE                 b3 = +0.451  p = 0.0479
  Month-year FE (primary)                 b3 = +1.241  p = 0.0000
  Adding year controls recovers the gradient signal.

TEST 3: post_mou main effect insufficient
  Month-of-year + post_mou dummy: interaction b3 = +0.209 (p=0.3315)
  A single dummy cannot substitute for year-level FE.

CONCLUSION:
  Month-year FE absorbs year-specific level shifts in deaths that are
  unrelated to weather (policy changes, crossing volumes, SAR coverage).
  Without absorbing these, the SWH x post_mou interaction conflates the
  gradient change with the level change. Month-year FE is necessary.
